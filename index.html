An animal shelter or pound is a place where stray, lost, abandoned or surrendered animals — mostly dogs and cats — are housed.

  • Shelter partners include rescue groups, fosters and sanctuaries.
  • An animal in a shelter has four outcomes: return to owner, adoption, transfer to another shelter or rescue facility.
  • Many shelters routinely spay or neuter all their adoptable animals and vaccinate them for rabies and other routine pet diseases.
